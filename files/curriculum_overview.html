Curriculum Overview

Our Curriculum Overview (PDF) explains in detail our philosophy, teaching methods, and developmental focus. Below you’ll find a summary of what’s inside.

Philosophy

Washington University Nursery School supports each child's individual development through a play-based, Constructivist curriculum that focuses on the whole child's social, emotional, intellectual, and physical growth. Guided by developmental theorists, the school fosters a nurturing environment where children learn through their own interests and interactions with their environment.

Areas of Development

  • Social/Emotional: Collaboration, problem-solving, self-expression
  • Intellectual: Language, early math, science exploration
  • Physical: Gross motor (climbers, trikes, playground games) and fine motor (writing, drawing, manipulatives)
  • Visual Arts: Creative exploration with paint, clay, collage, and 3D constructions

How We Teach

  • Hands-on, multi-sensory learning experiences
  • Learning centers in art, writing, science, dramatic play, blocks, and literacy
  • Respectful questioning and independent problem-solving
  • Celebrating diversity through multicultural activities

Assessment & Communication

  • Teachers observe and adapt curriculum based on students’ needs
  • Parent-teacher conferences held twice a year
  • Ongoing documentation and feedback shared with families
